Professional Profile

As a researcher, I interrogate organizational change through intersectional and critical theoretical perspectives, investigating how institutions navigate complex transformational initiatives while grappling with deeply embedded power relations. My research agenda examines the political dimensions of organizational becoming, with particular attention to anti-racist change processes, sensemaking, paradoxes, and resistance in both formal and informal organizational contexts. My dissertation explores how organizations proclaim and navigate anti-racist change, examining the constitutive role of power in organizational becoming and surfacing how various stakeholders make sense of and experience these complex change processes.

Awards & Recognition

I am an American Association of University Women (AAUW) International Fellow, 2023-2024 ($25,000). Currently, I am completing my Ph.D. in Business Administration (Organizations and Social Change) at the University of Massachusetts Boston's College of Management. I earned my MSc in Prosperity, Innovation, and Entrepreneurship with Distinction from University College London (UCL), where my dissertation on employee-driven green human resource management received the Best Dissertation Award. I also hold a Bachelor of Commerce (Human Resource Management, First Class Honors) and Diploma in Integrated Business Studies (Distinction) from the University for Development Studies, Ghana.

I have received over $272,000 in fellowships and scholarships, including the American Association of University Women (AAUW) International Fellowship ($25,000), The Dean Fellowships, Commonwealth Shared Scholarship (£48,100), and multiple research grants from UMass Boston including RJC Proposal Award ($19,500), Faculty and Staff Union Grant ($4,000), and Summer Researcher Grant ($3,000).

Academic honors include: CMS Division (AOM) Most Promising Early Career Paper (2024), Ph.D. Award for Academic Distinction in Organizations and Social Change (UMass Boston, 2023), Beacon Emerging Leader Award (UMass Boston, 2023), Best Graduating Student (University for Development Studies, 2019), RAINS Cash Prize for First Female to attain First Class Honors (2019), and Overall Best Diploma Graduating Student (2016).

Scholarly Practice
