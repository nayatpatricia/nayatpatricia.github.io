Biography

Scholarship at the intersection of power, justice, & transformation

Patricia studies what happens when organizations promise justice — how those promises are interpreted, experienced, and managed over time, and what these processes reveal about power and the reproduction of inequality.

She earned her PhD in Business Administration (Organization & Social Change) from the University of Massachusetts Boston in 2026, with a dissertation on The Anti-Racist Organization? Power, Embodiment, and the Politics of Becoming. She also holds an MSc from University College London and a Bachelor of Commerce from the University for Development Studies, Ghana.

Her work has appeared in Academy of Management Learning & Education (A*), Organization (A), and Edward Elgar Publishing. She is a CMS Division (AOM) Most Promising Early Career Paper recipient (2024), an AAUW International Fellow, and a Commonwealth Shared Scholar.

She is founder and CEO of Dream Alliance, and has taught at Northeastern University, Bentley University, and UMass Boston.
