Research & Publications

I study what happens when organizations promise justice — examining how those promises are interpreted, contested, and managed over time, and what these processes reveal about the persistence of power and inequality.

Organization ABDC Ranking: A 2025

Critical Conversations in MOS: A Dual-Interview on the Continuing Struggles Against Anti-Blackness and Racial Capitalism

Nkomo, S., & Naya, P. T.

Organization, 32(2), pp. 180–190

A dual-interview with Professor Stella Nkomo on anti-Blackness in Management and Organization Studies, examined through racial capitalism and Black feminist thought. Traces how anti-Blackness is reproduced through organizational theory and practice, and what that demands of scholars committed to change.

Academy of Management Learning & Education ABDC Ranking: A* 2025

"Those of Us Who Stay": Sensemaking, Embodiment, and Navigation of the Inclusion–Exclusion Paradox

Naya, T. P., Contu, A., & Poole, M. J.

Academy of Management Learning & Education, Vol. 24, No. 3, pp. 373–396

Examines how Black and Brown women academics make sense of being simultaneously included and excluded in university settings. Drawing on embodiment theory and organizational paradox, it shows the emotional and intellectual labor of navigating institutions that claim inclusion while reproducing inequity.

Academy of Management Learning & Education ABDC Ranking: A* AOM Early Career Award 2024 2024

Piercing the Veil of Colorblindness with an Anti-Racist Identity: Sensemaking, Power, and the Reproduction of Racialized Socioeconomic Inequality

Naya, T. P., Contu, A., & Poole, M. J.

Academy of Management Learning & Education, 23(3), pp. 387–407

Analyzes how anti-racist commitments at a business school are interpreted and contested by students and faculty. Drawing on sensemaking theory and critical race scholarship, it shows how colorblind ideology lets institutions narrate progress while sustaining racialized hierarchies. Awarded the CMS Division's Most Promising Early Career Paper at the 2024 Academy of Management Annual Meeting, Chicago.

UCL Institute for Global Prosperity Working Paper · 2022

The Era of Inclusivity: Employees Driving Green Human Resource Management toward Sustainable Prosperity

Naya, T. P.

UCL Institute for Global Prosperity Working Paper

Drawing on the MSc Best Dissertation (UCL, 2021), examines how employee agency shapes organizational sustainability through Green Human Resource Management. Proposes a model of GHRM in which workers — not only executives — drive ecological and social responsibility.

Edward Elgar Publishing Accepted · Forthcoming

The Currency of Racism: How Routine Organizational Processes Sustain Racial Inequality

Naya, T. P.

In A Research Agenda for Diversity Management. Edward Elgar Publishing Ltd.

Argues that racial inequality persists not through exceptional acts of prejudice but through routine organizational processes. Drawing on critical race theory, examines how hiring criteria, performance metrics, mentorship, and informal networks function as currency — accessible to those who already hold racial capital, and out of reach for those who don't.

Edward Elgar Publishing 2026

Embracing Colourbraveness: A Sankofa Approach to Decolonising Business Schools

Naya, T. P.

In The Elgar Companion to Management Education and the Sustainable Development Goals, pp. 127–137. Edward Elgar Publishing.

Proposes colourbraveness — a deliberate engagement with race — as a counterpoint to colourblindness in management education. Drawing on the Akan concept of Sankofa (learning from the past to move forward), offers a decolonial framework for how business schools produce and suppress racial knowledge through curriculum, faculty, and institutional culture.
